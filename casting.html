Casting

Voice Cast

Voices chosen for performance, not profile. Tested with children, validated by experts, and deliberately built to let the show speak for itself.

The Voices

The voices of Wiggle Wood were chosen for one reason above all others: they work. Every principal cast member was selected for the quality and distinctiveness of their performance, not the size of their profile. The pilot episode has been tested extensively with children and parents, and the feedback has been unambiguous: the voices are exceptional.

Early years consultant Dr Jacqueline Harding, one of the UK’s leading authorities on child development and media, reviewed the pilot in full. Her advice was clear: “Don’t change a thing. You can’t improve on this.” We are taking that advice seriously. The voices stay.

A Deliberate Choice: No Big Celebrity Names

This was not a default. It was a decision. Wiggle Wood has deliberately moved away from the idea of anchoring the show to a headline celebrity name. The data, and the viewing habits of our audience, tell us why.

Children do not care about celebrity. A three-year-old does not know who a pop star is, and does not need to. What they respond to is warmth, distinctiveness, and emotional truth in a voice, and that is exactly what our cast delivers.

Parents, meanwhile, are not reached through celebrity alone. They are reached through trust, through community, and through the parent influencer networks that shape what families actually watch. Just because someone is a celebrity does not mean they have parents following them. Our influencer marketing strategy is built around the people who do.

It is also worth noting that the most successful competitor shows in the preschool space did not launch on the back of big names. The show has to stand on its own, and ours does. The pilot results confirm it.

Voice Director - Dave Peacock

Emmy-Award winning Dave Peacock has been voice directing and casting professionally for almost 20 years. Specialising in animation, he is one of the most in-demand voice directors in the UK, having directed over 70 TV series and a dozen features - including Hilda, Thunderbirds Are Go!, Moominvalley, The Octonauts, Dennis & Gnasher Unleashed, Love, Death & Robots and Go Jetters. He was Emmy-nominated for his voice direction on Hilda in 2021 and 2022, and won an Emmy in 2022 as Supervising Dialogue Editor on Octonauts: Ring of Fire.

Principal Voice Cast

Every principal cast member was chosen for performance quality and vocal distinctiveness. All have been tested with children and received with exceptional results. The voices are confirmed and will not change.

Chadwick
Corinna Brown (Heartstopper, Netflix)
Corinna’s lively South London accent infuses the character with humour, energy and infectious enthusiasm. Her performance captures Chadwick’s bold spirit and big-hearted personality, making him an instant favourite with young audiences.
Cosmo
Daisy Sequerra (Belgravia: The Next Chapter, ITV)
Daisy brings a bright, matter of fact Yorkshire accent to the role. Her grounded delivery captures Cosmo’s endearing honesty, assertive confidence and sharp observational wit, perfectly reflecting her thoughtful, observant and very practical nature.
Cyd
Blanche Anderson (Viewfinder, PlayStation)
Blanche gives Cyd a soft, whimsical Edinburgh accent that adds warmth and gentle curiosity to the character. Her performance gives Cyd a dreamy charm, bringing a playful sensitivity to the Wiggle Wood world.
Bellona Butterfly
Naomi McDonald
Naomi brings warmth, authority and genuine emotional depth to Bellona. Her performance in the pilot was so well received by children, parents, and our creative consultants alike that the decision was straightforward: she is Bellona.
Luna Glow-worm
Ellie Wallwork (Doctor Who, BBC1)
Ellie brings a beautifully balanced performance to the role. She captures Luna’s teenage mix of uncertainty and attitude, conveying both her lack of confidence in glow and her quietly determined spirit. Her delivery blends vulnerability with flashes of dry, slightly exasperated humour, perfectly reflecting Luna’s journey towards believing in herself.
Robin Robin
Shash Hira (Thomas Edison’s Secret Lab, Kartoon Channel)
Shash transforms the character into an endearingly theatrical, slightly grumpy elder of the woods. With the ‘Thespian’ air of a retired stage actor, he gives Robin a wonderfully expressive presence, blending dry wit with surprising tenderness.

Guest Voice Cast

Each episode introduces a new guest character, voiced by a rotating cast of talent. Rather than chasing headline celebrity names, our guest casting strategy focuses on parent-facing influencers and trusted voices with genuine followings among families. Being well-known does not automatically mean being well-followed by parents, and it is parents who decide what their children watch. Guest voices will be selected for their reach within parenting communities, providing repeatable PR moments that connect with the right audience at the right moment.
