Music

Music as a Market Differentiator

Led by Banks & Wag, the UK’s foremost children’s music duo, original music is woven into the storytelling fabric of every single episode.

Led by Banks & Wag, the UK’s leading children’s music composing and songwriting duo, original music is a core differentiator of Wiggle Wood.

Disneyesque music is embedded into the storytelling fabric of the series. Each episode features a musical moment that is either emotionally driven, helping characters process feelings, or action-based, supporting play, adventure, movement, or imagination. This musical structure deepens emotional engagement, reinforces learning, and creates a distinctive storytelling rhythm that sets Wiggle Wood apart within the preschool landscape.

Wiggle Wood adopts a deliberate songbook strategy, designed specifically for the way young children engage with music. Each season features a curated album of 10 original songs that recur across the season’s 52 episodes, allowing children to hear the same songs repeatedly and build familiarity. For preschool audiences, repetition drives attachment, participation, and memorisation, meaning songs quickly become recognisable anchors within the Wiggle Wood world.

Composers & Songwriters – Banks & Wag

Banks & Wag are one of the most established and commercially successful composing and songwriting duos in UK children’s music. Specialists in crafting melodic, high-quality pop music for young audiences, they have delivered multiple number one hits in the children’s music charts alongside award-winning broadcast credits. Best known for their work on flagship series including Go Jetters, ZingZillas, Blue Peter, Newsround and Mighty Little Bheem, Banks & Wag are the natural home for a series that puts music at the very heart of its storytelling.

Their deep understanding of what resonates with preschool audiences – and what parents will happily hear on repeat – makes them an exceptional fit for Wiggle Wood’s ambition to build a songbook that is both emotionally powerful and commercially scalable.

Background

Chris Banks and Wag Marshall-Page studied Commercial Music together at the University of Westminster (1996–1999). After graduation, Chris studied Composition For Screen at the Royal College of Music (1999–2000), continuing to perform with Wag as members of indie band Nnook. The group disbanded in 1999, and the pair went on to work with pop acts including McFly, Sugababes, Busted, Blue, and Sophie Ellis-Bextor; and Wag joined Infadels as bassist.

As composers, they have written for The X Factor, ZingZillas, Year Dot, Strictly Dance Fever, The National Lottery Awards, Ross Noble, Dead Ringers, and Spain’s Tele Madrid News, winning a Silver Broadcast Design Award for Best News Theme.

Their title song for How To Start Your Own Country was described as “brilliant” by the Radio Times, “dangerously catchy” by Bella Online, and “worthy of Top of the Pops” by the Daily Mirror. The series went on to win two BAFTAs and spawned an iTunes #1 single in 2005.

Banks was also behind the CBeebies series Space Pirates, whose theme tune topped the iTunes Children’s Chart for 11 weeks in 2007–2008.

In 2010, they wrote and produced all the music for the CBeebies series ZingZillas. On 30 August 2010, the series’ first single “Do You Didgeridoo?” was released, reaching #1 in the iTunes Children’s Chart.

The Songs

Two original songs have already been written and will anchor the first season.

Wiggle It Out

The series’ signature track and the one constant across every season of Wiggle Wood. A joyful, irresistibly catchy pop anthem in the spirit of Walking on Sunshine, it is the kind of song that makes children physically unable to sit still. It arrives at the same point in every episode, around one minute in, as a 45-second musical highlight that spotlights each Wiggler in turn with their own signature wiggle move. Its fixed position in the format is intentional: children quickly learn it is coming, which builds anticipation and makes the moment feel like a joyful ritual rather than simply a song. Wiggle It Out is designed to become one of those songs that children ask to hear again the moment it ends, and that parents find themselves humming for the rest of the day.

Best is Beautiful

Written for the pilot episode, Show Your Glow. Cyd worries that the present she has made for Bellona Butterfly simply is not good enough. Then she meets Luna Glow-worm, who carries the same quiet fear about her own glow. As the two connect, something shifts: Cyd sees that Luna’s glow is genuinely beautiful, even though Luna cannot see it herself. And in that moment, she wonders whether her own present might be more beautiful than she realised. It is Bellona who brings the episode home, singing Your Best is Beautiful to the Wigglers in a moment of pure warmth and reassurance. “It is written in the stars to be proud of who you are, the magic that you make is true. Your best is beautiful.” The song is a feel-good ballad, closer in spirit to Christina Aguilera’s Beautiful than to a pop anthem, but warmer, more hopeful, and designed to leave children and parents alike with their hearts a little fuller. Where Wiggle It Out gets children on their feet, Best is Beautiful brings them close. Together, the two songs establish the emotional range that will define the Wiggle Wood songbook.

The Luna Rap

An original rap performed by Chadwick in the pilot episode to encourage Luna Glow-worm to move, dance, and let her glow shine. These shorter musical moments reflect the spontaneity and playfulness of the Wiggle Wood world, ensuring music feels embedded in the characters rather than performed for the audience. Each episode also closes with a song, giving the storytelling a musical resolution that reinforces the emotional message of the episode.

Music as a Commercial Engine

Beyond these anchor songs, this approach also creates a scalable music catalogue. Each season contributes ten new tracks to the Wiggle Wood songbook, building a growing library of original music that extends beyond the episodes themselves across streaming platforms, music videos, and live experiences. As seasons accumulate, the catalogue becomes an increasingly valuable long-term asset within the wider Wiggle Wood IP portfolio.

Music also plays an important role in encouraging family co-viewing. Wiggle Wood’s songs are designed to be uplifting, contemporary, and enjoyable for both children and adults. The musical tone draws inspiration from feel-good pop anthems such as Walking on Sunshine by Katrina and the Waves, Can’t Stop the Feeling by Justin Timberlake, and Happy by Pharrell Williams. This helps ensure the music energises families rather than becoming repetitive background noise, extending the appeal beyond the core 3 to 5 audience to include siblings and parents.

Crucially, music is not just a creative feature. It is a proven commercial engine within children’s IP and an important long-term rights asset. Wiggle Wood will build and retain ownership of its original song catalogue, enabling monetisation across streaming platforms, publishing, sync licensing, soundtrack albums, music videos, and live singalong events. The scale of opportunity is well illustrated by Baby Shark, the most viewed video in YouTube history. Meanwhile, songs such as Can We Fix It from Bob the Builder have generated significant royalties across broadcast, music sales, and licensing.

By intentionally building a repeatable catalogue from the outset, Wiggle Wood positions music as a key engine of brand longevity, emotional connection, and commercial scalability, helping transform the series into a multi-platform entertainment franchise with enduring cultural and commercial value.
