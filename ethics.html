Ethics

Values

A purpose-driven series committed to emotional, social and environmental wellbeing - built on best practice, transparency and child-centred storytelling.

Best Practice and Production Transparency

Wiggle Wood is being built on a foundation of best practice and full production transparency. The series will be bonded through Paterson James, ensuring that a fully delivered series is guaranteed and offering investors confidence that the production will be completed to specification, regardless of circumstance.

The HMRC Animation Tax Credit will be applied with integrity, both to maximise UK-based production spend and to reinvest in the UK’s next generation of creative talent. In line with industry expectations, two percent of the tax credit will be directed back into the industry, supporting the development of emerging professionals. Wiggle Wood will work closely with ScreenSkills UK, the industry body for skills and training across the screen industries, and Creative Access, a charity dedicated to helping young people from underrepresented backgrounds build careers in the creative industries. Together, these partnerships ensure that Wiggle Wood’s success contributes meaningfully to a more diverse and sustainable creative sector.

All revenues will be managed via the independent collections agency Fintage House, ensuring full financial oversight and industry-standard royalty distribution. At every level, Wiggle Wood is being produced to the highest possible standards: commercially rigorous, creatively ambitious, and ethically sound.

Ethical Integrity & Child-Centred Storytelling

At its core, Wiggle Wood is a purpose-driven series committed to emotional, social, and environmental wellbeing. Every episode is crafted with educational integrity and designed to support healthy emotional development, positive relationships, and age-appropriate curiosity about the natural world. Aligned with YouTube’s Made for Kids policies and traditional broadcaster standards, all content is thoroughly reviewed to ensure compliance with editorial guidelines, safeguarding principles, and best practices in early years storytelling.

There is no imitable content, no overstimulation, and no reliance on fast-cut tactics. Instead, Wiggle Wood promotes calm engagement, positive role modelling, and inclusive values, underpinned by a thoughtful editorial framework that prioritises safety, empathy, and meaningful entertainment. The series aims not only to delight but to do so responsibly, helping young viewers make sense of their world in a way that is enriching, respectful, and developmentally appropriate.

Wiggle Wood’s child development adviser Dr Jacqueline Harding, an international child development expert, former BBC Education Editor, and Government Adviser on children’s media and education, is a key part of the writing team, ensuring every story is age-appropriate and supportive of young audiences’ developmental and emotional needs.

Carbon Literacy and Responsibility

Wiggle Wood’s sustainability commitment is embedded across the full production and operational life of the business, not limited to the screen. The series will be produced in accordance with Albert, BAFTA’s sustainability framework, with a carbon report produced for each episode and series. Beyond production, the business will operate on green energy, apply circular economy principles to how it runs and grows, and hold itself accountable to independent standards across environmental performance, diversity and inclusion, and responsible employment.

To support this, Wiggle Wood will be enrolling with FuturePlus, an independent sustainability platform that works similarly to B Corp certification in its ethos. FuturePlus holds organisations to a framework of continuous, verified improvement rather than a one-time credential, providing a transparent and independently tracked record of progress across the areas that matter most to families, partners, and investors.

This commitment will be led by Mark Downes, one of the UK’s most respected figures in sustainable media production. Mark is Chief Storyteller at Green Eyed Monster Films, Head of Content for the Earth Prize, a member of the Expert Council for Open ESG, and former Head of Sustainability at Ecoflix. A career highlight was producing a trilogy of films with Sir David Attenborough for Generation Ocean. Sustainability is not a credential Mark has added to a filmmaking career. It is his life’s work.

Diversity and Inclusion

Diversity and inclusion, both behind and in front of the camera, is integral to the Wiggle Wood production. Within the series itself, representation is woven into the storytelling from the outset. Chadwick, one of the central characters, is a wheelchair user, supported by specialist disability adviser Ally Castle MBE, a former BBC strategist awarded an MBE for services to inclusion in broadcasting. The series also reflects the breadth and richness of regional UK voices, with Scottish representation among its core characters.

The cast, writing, production, and animation team reflects diversity across gender, disability, race, ethnicity, and socio-economic background. The production is also committed to supporting emerging talent at both ends of the career spectrum, offering mentorship opportunities alongside the senior team.

Wiggle Wood’s approach to inclusion is embedded within the creative process rather than treated as an afterthought. Wiggle Wood believes that diverse teams and authentic representation strengthen storytelling and ensure that children from a wide range of backgrounds can see themselves reflected in the world of Wiggle Wood.

Self-Worth

Wiggle Wood is built on the belief that every child deserves to feel seen, valued, and loved exactly as they are. This conviction runs through every episode and extends beyond the stories themselves. Each episode closes with a short, simple mindfulness moment, inviting young audiences to pause, breathe, and appreciate something about themselves or the world around them.

When children watch Wiggle Wood, they feel safe. They feel accepted. They feel that this is a world they belong in. And when parents watch alongside them, they feel something equally powerful: pride, warmth, and the quiet reassurance that what their child is watching is genuinely good for them. That combination, a child who feels seen and a parent who feels at ease, is what Wiggle Wood is designed to create, and it is the foundation on which lasting audience loyalty is built.

Positive Role Models

Every Wiggle Wood character is a role model, not because they are perfect, but because they are self-aware. Characters make mistakes, take responsibility, and make things right. Kindness is at the heart of every story, and behaviour that falls short of that standard is always acknowledged and addressed. Children watching Wiggle Wood see a world where trying to do better is always worthwhile.

A wholesome, music-led animated series promoting kindness, diversity and inclusion. A brand built to last.
