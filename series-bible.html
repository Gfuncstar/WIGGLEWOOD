The Series Bible

The creative foundation every team member reads first

The Wiggle Wood Series Bible is the single document that holds the world together. It is the first thing anyone joining the creative team reads, and the reference every writer, director, and designer returns to throughout production.

It defines how the characters think, how they speak, and how they behave. It sets out what happens in Wiggle Wood and, just as importantly, what does not. It gives every new voice the context they need to contribute to the world without pulling it in the wrong direction.

As the series grows, the bible grows with it. When a new character is introduced, when the world extends into a new location, when a storytelling rule is refined in production, the bible is updated to reflect it. It is a living document, not a fixed one.

“Anyone new to the project reads the bible and understands the world. The characters, the rules, the tone: everything that unites the storytelling.”

What the Bible Covers

Five areas. One unified world.

The bible is organised around the five things every creative team member needs to understand before they write a single word or draw a single frame.

1
The World

The woodland setting, the five core locations, the rules of the physical space, and the atmosphere that makes Wiggle Wood feel unlike anywhere else in children's television.

2
The Characters

Full profiles for Cyd, Cosmo, Chadwick, and Bellona: how they think, how they speak, what drives them, what they fear, and how they relate to one another. The bible is the source of truth for every character decision.

3
Tone & Voice

The emotional register of the series: warm, unhurried, and genuinely funny without ever being frantic. What the series sounds like, and what it never sounds like.

4
The Rules

The creative guardrails that protect the world. What Wiggle Wood permits and what it does not: for storytelling, for character behaviour, for the visual world, and for the music.

5
The Long-term Vision

How the series grows across seasons, into brand extensions, and into new formats, while keeping the world consistent and the characters true to themselves.

A Living Document

Built to grow with the series

The bible is not a document written once and filed away. It is updated at every significant stage of production: when the world extends, when a character develops, when a creative decision is made that sets a new precedent.

“The bible is what keeps every writer, director, and designer working from the same world: the same characters, the same rules, the same vision. It is what makes Wiggle Wood feel consistent whether you joined the project at the beginning or are joining it now.”

Aimée Anderson, Creator of Wiggle Wood
