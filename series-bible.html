Overview

A groundbreaking musical animation

WIGGLE WOOD is a truly independent production and a groundbreaking musical animation. But most important of all, it’s home to three adventurous young caterpillars named Cyd, Chadwick & Cosmo.

Just like our target audience of 3– to 5–year–olds, these little ‘Wigglers’ are taking their first steps into an exciting world, learning how to manage their emotions, meeting new friends and uncovering nature’s best kept secrets.

Guided by Bellona, their guardian butterfly, the Wigglers discover that it’s their differences that make them successful — and that being unique can be their very own superpower… just so long as they can work together.

The series is a modern take on Aesop’s fables, where every adventure offers a mirror into Cyd, Chadwick and Cosmo’s own lives. By helping others, they develop their own skills and learn to navigate their emotions, abilities and friendships.

Children and families will come for the comedy and music, but they will stay for the lovable characters and valuable insights into their own emotional well-being.

Meet the Cast

The Wigglers & their world

All three of our little caterpillars are inquisitive and resourceful, but they each have a very different approach that doesn’t always gel with one another, making their friendships tricky to navigate.

Lead Wiggler
Cyd
Innovative / Procrastinator

A thoughtful dreamer, Cyd (pronounced ‘Sid’) doesn’t just think outside the box — she imagines a world where there isn’t one. She approaches problems from a completely unexpected angle, and her solutions are equally convoluted… but they work… sort of.

She is the most emotionally aware Wiggler and always considers her friends’ feelings, so much so that she overthinks everything. In her mind, no problem is too big, but she must work through every possible outcome before she fixes it.

“She paints with a broad brush and dances like NOBODY is watching.”

Likes: Mysteries, puzzles, big ideas and Wiggle-pops (popcorn)
Dislikes: Feeling rushed, failure — because everything has to be perfect
Signature move: Back spin — it looks cool and helps her see the world from a different angle
“Slow and steady… leaves more time for fun!”
Lead Wiggler
Cosmo
Proactive / Impatient

Determined, knowledgeable and never a minute late, Cosmo is a natural leader who always says what’s on her mind and has everything planned out before the others have even found their shoes.

She is older than the other Wigglers (if only by a few minutes) and loves to remind Cyd and Chadwick that she’s been around the branch a couple more times than them. She can be very proactive, but her single-mindedness often causes her to race past the emotional and magical moments along the way.

“She paints with a fine brush and dances like EVERYONE is watching.”

Likes: Buttercup nectar, plants and ecology, giving a good lecture
Dislikes: Waste, inefficiency, thoughtlessness that impacts the natural world
Signature move: The belly buster — a caterpillar version of ‘the worm’
“If you want something done properly…”
Lead Wiggler
Chadwick
Headstrong / Emotional

Chadwick is the high-energy, high-spirited joker of the group. Born without back legs, Chadwick uses a wheelchair, but he is always first to the finish line. Fast-talking, fast-moving, and independent, he is driven by instinct, confidence, and a love of making people laugh.

His emotions run at full volume. When things go well, it is the best day ever. When they do not, it is a total disaster, at least in his mind. Passionate, messy, and a bit impulsive, he is the most expressive of the Wigglers.

“He paints with a roller and dances like EVERYONE SHOULD be watching!”

Likes: Practical jokes (especially peekaboo), racing, and blueberry juice
Dislikes: Being ignored, coming last, rainy days (if he has to stay inside)
Signature move: Caterpillar Curl — rolls into a ball and backspins like a pinball
“Someone needs a hug!”  •  “Race you… I won!”
Guardian Butterfly
Bellona
Caring / Nurturing

Bellona is the warm, wise heart of Wiggle Wood. Calm, compassionate, and endlessly kind, she helps the Wigglers reflect, grow, and find their own way. Like a cool big sister, she is always nearby but never overbearing, giving the Wigglers space to ‘wiggle out’ problems for themselves without ever making them feel led.

She seems to know everyone in Wiggle Wood. Through her many friendships, we glimpse her own colourful past, reminding us that even the wisest butterflies were once little wigglers finding their way in the world.

Above all, Bellona loves a good song and dance — because she knows that there is no problem so big that it can’t be solved with a big production number… and this is one butterfly that knows how to rock!

Likes: Mentoring, learning, growth, singing and dancing
Signature move: The Butterfly Flutterby — an airborne pirouette that captures her grace, energy, and joy
“What can we learn?”  •  “Tell us your story.”  •  “You teach me.”

Recurring & Guest Characters

The wider world of Wiggle Wood

Each episode introduces a woodland resident or creature passing through whose problem mirrors what the Wigglers are experiencing — giving the audience empathy and insight through a fresh perspective.

Luna Glow-worm

Shy, sweet, and quietly determined. A glow-worm with stage fright who doesn’t believe her glow is good enough. Luna is the kind of character children and parents identify with and want to protect — already a series fan favourite.

Robin Robin

Theatrical, opinionated, and ever so slightly pompous. Robin carries himself with the air of a retired stage actor who has never quite retired. Tender beneath his bluster, and endlessly entertaining.

Oliver Owl

Robin Robin’s nocturnal neighbour. Oliver loves to DJ through the night with the joy and innocence of someone who has never once been told to keep it down. He means no harm. He simply has no idea what time it is.

Flora the Honeybee

Always busy, always buzzing, and never quite stopping long enough to think things through. Flora means well, but her relentless busyness is often the cause of the very problems she’s too busy to notice.

Nancy Weaver

A mischievous spider with a genuine gift for spinning stories. Nancy is not exactly dishonest — she just has a very creative relationship with the truth.

Admiral Butterfly

A gruff old friend of Bellona’s who is too proud to ask for help. His grumpy exterior hides a friendly soul who is desperate for a friend.

Episode Format

A repeatable emotional journey

Every 10-minute episode of Wiggle Wood follows the same carefully considered structure — a repeatable emotional journey designed to resonate with preschool children and build genuine developmental value through storytelling, music, and reflection.

1
The Emotional Trigger

Each episode begins with one or more of the Wigglers experiencing a moment of emotional difficulty: worry, frustration, anxiety, anger, or uncertainty. The episode begins where the child already is.

2
Wiggle It Out

Rather than moving straight into action, the Wigglers pause and wiggle it out — a recurring musical centrepiece: an uplifting, transitional music video moment in which the characters sing, move, and release their feelings.

3
The Guest Character

In a new location, the Wigglers encounter a woodland resident facing a problem that mirrors their own. Children learn through empathy, not instruction. Success is not always guaranteed, but teamwork, empathy, and listening lead the Wigglers closer to what really matters.

4
Return to Caterpillar Cubby

The Wigglers return home and reflect on what they have experienced. The episode closes with a reflective song that reinforces the emotional arc of the story, often led by Bellona. The emotional lesson is gently consolidated — never prescribed.

5
The Mindfulness Moment

Every episode ends with a short mindfulness segment during the credits, offering simple, age-appropriate meditations focused on self-worth, calm, and gratitude — designed to ease the transition toward bedtime.

Music

The series USP is its musical heart

Wiggle Wood will feature many original songs, some written by Musical Director Jax Jones, others by children’s songwriting duo Banks & Wag. Music unites broader demographics and encourages co-viewing.

The timeless songs throughout the series will enable Wiggle Wood to be a show that is ‘hard to grow out of’ — enabling parents and carers to enjoy it with their children, instead of being tormented by repetitive, annoying songs.

Each episode features three musical moments:

1
Wiggle Wood Rock (working title)

The opening theme song — an inclusive introduction to the series and our characters, welcoming everyone to Wiggle Wood, where no problem is too big and no hero is too small.

2
Wiggle It Out

The recurring pop song that encourages the Wigglers and the audience to shake away their worries and find a solution. A foot-stomping, heart-pounding call to action that will get children and families up and moving.

3
Reflection Song

Led by Bellona, this big musical number wraps the story up, allowing characters and audience to reflect on what they have learned. Ten different reflection songs, themed around a different emotional lesson, will be repeated across the series.

The World

The world of Wiggle Wood

Wiggle Wood is a traditional woodland, home to a huge and diverse population of insects, arachnids, reptiles, birds and small mammals. A natural, temperate environment broadly situated in the UK — though this is never stated in the series. As such, the world has seasons, offering a huge variety of stories to tell.

Some creatures live in the woods and some are just passing through — which means the Wigglers can bump into a spider collecting dew from her web, a flamingo who has lost her way, or even a giant polar bear who took a winter holiday but now needs to find shade from the spring sunshine. No matter who these animals are or where they come from, they all share one thing in common: they need help from the Wigglers.

The seasons affect the inhabitants of Wiggle Wood, and the Wigglers will come across friends old and new at different stages in their seasonal life cycle — a character they may have previously helped during the spring months will face different challenges during the autumn as they prepare for the coming cold weather, allowing us to revisit audience favourites.

This also provides opportunities for seasonal specials that tie into those tent-pole moments in kids’ lives — from typical spring, summer, autumn and winter activities to those based in the animal kingdom, such as hibernation, migration, flood, drought, growth and rebirth.

“A big, wide world, seen through the lens of a small child. Full of wonder, because nature is magical and there is a lesson to be learned from every creature.”

Locations

An ever-expanding world

New locations will be introduced through the series. The following stand out as our starting points.

Caterpillar Cubby

At the centre of the woods stands the Great Oak, and nestled in the nook of one of its 700-year-old branches is Caterpillar Cubby. The home, school and safe place for Bellona to teach her Wigglers — a hollow tree branch that has naturally grown into the shape of a caterpillar, featuring a bedroom, living space, play-space and greenhouse.

Wiggle Wood Way

The main highway through the woods runs right past the Great Oak and brings a diverse range of visitors to the area. Rather than a busy street, this is a forest track — with travellers hiking or racing along on their own natural transportation.

Pebble Brook

A slow-running stream that passes by the Great Oak and leads off through the woods towards a lake in the meadow. Shallow enough for the Wigglers to play near safely — racing model leaf boats and splashing in the sunshine.

Midsummer Meadow

A vibrant flower meadow, home to a bustling population of small animals and insects just waiting for the Wigglers to lend them a helping hand. Long grasses and bright flowers sway in a warm breeze.

Lily Pad Lake

A pond near the meadow that looks like a giant lake to the little Wigglers. Here, they can enjoy lakeside adventures, safe on the floating lily pads — and travel across the lake to go waterskiing with the pond-skaters.

Design & Animation

Look and feel

Wiggle Wood is realised in 2.5D animation — a process where 2D characters are given a degree of shading and weight, while the backgrounds have a depth of field and dynamic lighting that suggests a real-world backdrop to the Wigglers’ adventures.

The characters are designed to feel aspirational, offering a little more emotional range than competing brands, with colour schemes that reflect the Wigglers’ distinct personalities. A large proportion of the emotional storytelling will come through facial expressions and body language to ensure the stories can be understood by all ages and abilities.

The characters’ unique silhouettes make them instantly recognisable: Cyd is round and cuddly, Cosmo is long and wiggly, Chadwick is compact and speedy — but they also have a commonality in their design that makes them feel part of a team.

Caterpillars are naturally fuzzy and the heroes look very soft and huggable. The audience should imagine they can reach into the screen and squeeze their new friends, just as they would their favourite toy at home — or even their very own plush Wiggler.

The Wigglers love to sing and dance, so their bodies have a lot of movement, allowing them to flex and perform as a child would. All movements will be replicable in the real world.

Design Philosophy

“This is the antithesis to over-stimulation. Think: accessible calm action and comedy. A family comedy where pacing and tone is appropriate for the youngest viewers, while layering in additional gags for older siblings and parents.”

Creative Guardrails

The rules of Wiggle Wood

The bible makes clear what the world permits and what it categorically does not, so that every story decision is made with confidence.

Wiggle Wood is…

  • A big, wide world, seen through the lens of a small child
  • Full of wonder, because nature is magical and there is a lesson to be learned from every creature
  • Where real-world challenges become supersized from the kids’ point of view
  • Where choices have consequences and plotlines are always driven by the Wigglers’ decisions
  • Where the laws of physics apply — there is no magic, beyond nature’s own
  • Where any creature can exist and is always in need of help from the Wigglers
  • A world without humans

Caterpillar Cubby is…

  • A safe place where children can reflect on their experiences and grow
  • A small pool where the tiniest change can create the biggest ripples
  • Where we don’t avoid difficult questions and age-appropriate behaviours
  • Where failure is nothing to be afraid of
  • Where differences and individuality are championed

The Wigglers are…

  • The audience’s best friends — they are real kids, with real joys, worries, interests and questions
  • Fallible and make all the same mistakes as real children, but they always learn from them
  • Messy — young caterpillars exploring their world and the relationships within it
  • A mirror for the audience — every child should identify with at least one character motivation in any given episode

Sample Storylines

Ten episodes developed, twenty springboards ready

Ten episodes are already developed to outline stage and ready to go into production. Below are three samples, alongside further springboards from the twenty already developed.

Episode 1

A Sticky Situation

Chadwick is determined to learn to play the bluebell horn, but he won’t accept anybody’s help — even though his failure to even make a sound with it is frustrating him. He becomes even more annoyed when Cosmo gives him a demonstration: she can play really loud.

On their way through the wood, the Wigglers come across Red Admiral Butterfly, perched on the side of a sycamore tree, unusually cagey about what he’s up to. Chadwick whizzes up to investigate and discovers the truth: the Admiral is stuck in some sticky sycamore sap — and now Chadwick is stuck too.

One by one the whole group gets stuck. The Admiral is contrite — he didn’t want to admit he needed help. He thought he could get out of this sticky situation on his own. Together they call for help with a mighty blast of Cosmo’s bluebell horn, drawing the attention of Barbara the Stag Beetle, who soon has everyone free. Back at Caterpillar Cubby, Bellona leads a song about it being OK to ask for help — and Chadwick finally finds the courage to ask Cosmo to teach him the horn.

Theme: It’s OK to ask for help.

Episode 2

What’s In a Name?

Cosmo is getting frustrated: Chadwick likes to call strawberries ‘dawbees’; Cyd calls them ‘red berries’. Cosmo just can’t understand why the others can’t use the right name. Bellona eases Cosmo onto a different topic by suggesting they head out to wiggle out their problems.

In the wood, they encounter Colin Woodlouse — Cosmo is delighted to share that woodlice have lots of funny nicknames. Colin sighs, because he absolutely does know this. Cosmo becomes increasingly animated listing the names, while Cyd and Chadwick try to mime what each one makes them think of. Colin finally runs out of patience and stomps off in a huff.

A gentle nudge from Bellona brings Cosmo to a revelation: she asks Colin which name he prefers. Colin smiles. “It’s… Colin!” Everyone laughs. Cosmo is happy to learn that everyone should respect what people choose to call themselves.

Theme: Respect what people choose to call themselves.

Episode 3

Funny Bug

Cyd and Cosmo are having fun making silly hats, but Chadwick is in a playful mood — all he wants to do is jump out on them shouting ‘Boo!’ It was kind of funny the first time, but all the boos are getting in the way of all-important hat-making. Bellona suggests Chadwick heads off into the wood to wiggle things out.

He encounters Simon Stick Insect, who has been masquerading as a twig. They begin a prank-off — but both friends soon begin to get frustrated with each other’s gags. Then Cyd and Cosmo arrive, wearing their fabulously silly hats. Chadwick and Simon can’t help but roll around laughing. Chadwick stops — Cyd and Cosmo managed to make them laugh without a prank! In a joyful music sequence, they discover different ways to make each other laugh: funny disguises, tickly vines, wiggly walks and silly faces.

Theme: There are lots of ways to make people laugh.

Further Springboards

Show Your Glow (pilot, full script available)

Luna the glow-worm is feeling shy about her shine. The Wigglers help her show her glow — and learn that your best is beautiful.

Don’t Yuck My Dung

Bruno the dung beetle needs help rolling his dungball, but Cosmo does not like the idea of touching poo. Bruno teaches the Wigglers that just because you don’t like something, it doesn’t mean everybody feels the same way. And that’s fine.

Hog Bugs

A gang of greedy locusts are passing through Wiggle Wood wanting to eat everything in sight. The Wigglers show that sharing has its own rewards.

Noisy Neighbours

Cosmo bumps into Oliver Owl, who’s at his wit’s end — his neighbour Robin Robin loves to sing, and nocturnal Oliver just wants to sleep. Maybe there’s a simpler solution than earplugs?

Strong as Silk

Chadwick can’t believe that Sansi Silkworm’s thread is strong enough to help. Our caterpillar friend needs to learn that looks can be deceiving and things — and people — can be stronger than they seem.
