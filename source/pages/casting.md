## Body copy
- In early years animation, the voices behind the characters may be less important to young viewers, but they matter greatly to their caregivers. Wiggle Wood’s casting approach combines the best of both worlds: endearing, distinctive vocal performances that captivate children, and high-recognition, talent-led names that drive parent engagement, PR, and social media reach.
- Wiggle Wood's flagship casting choice is Mel C, the preferred voice for Bellona Butterfly. Bellona serves as the warm-hearted, wise and gently whimsical narrator of Wiggle Wood, guiding the young caterpillars through moments of emotional uncertainty towards clarity and confidence. The role demands vocal warmth, musical excellence and genuine emotional credibility.
- Mel represents a strategically aligned choice on both creative and commercial grounds. As a member of the Spice Girls, one of the most successful and culturally influential girl groups of all time, she carries decades of global recognition and multi-generational appeal. Combined with her established solo career, she brings immediate cross-border awareness that directly supports Wiggle Wood’s day-and-date global YouTube launch strategy. Her profile extends well beyond the UK, reinforcing Wiggle Wood’s ambition to scale as an international preschool brand from inception.
- Creatively, she offers rare dual capability. An Olivier Awards nominee for her stunning performance in Blood Brothers, she combines proven acting credentials with exceptional vocal strength. Her soft Liverpudlian tone carries both warmth and authority, making her ideally suited to the nurturing, big-sister presence that Bellona embodies. In a music-led series anchored by original songs in every episode, this seamless ability to perform in both spoken narrative and song is a material advantage.
- Importantly, this is talent-led casting that enhances brand equity rather than distracts from it. Mel’s authenticity, relatability and grounded public persona align closely with Wiggle Wood’s core values of emotional resilience, kindness and self-belief. She brings global star recognition while retaining a family-safe, trusted voice.
- As Wiggle Wood moves towards production financing, this calibre of casting target signals the level at which it intends to operate: creatively, commercially, and internationally.
- The three main child characters - Cosmo, Cyd, and Chadwick - are voiced by emerging and established adult actors with strong voice acting skills within the preschool space.
- Cosmo is voiced by Daisy Sequerra (Belgravia: The Next Chapter, ITV) who brings a bright, matter of fact Yorkshire accent to the role. Daisy’s grounded delivery captures Cosmo’s endearing honesty, assertive confidence and sharp observational wit, perfectly reflecting her thoughtful, observant and very practical nature.
- Cyd is voiced by Blanche Anderson (Viewfinder, PlayStation) who gives Cyd a soft, whimsical Edinburgh accent that adds warmth and gentle curiosity to the character. Blanche’s performance gives Cyd a dreamy charm, bringing a playful sensitivity to the Wiggle Wood world.
- Chadwick is voiced by Corinna Brown (Heartstopper, Netflix) whose lively South London accent infuses the character with humour, energy and infectious enthusiasm. Corinna’s performance captures Chadwick’s bold spirit and big-hearted personality, making him an instant favourite with young audiences.
- Luna Glow-worm is voiced by Ellie Wallwork (Doctor Who, BBC1) who brings a beautifully balanced performance to the role. Ellie captures Luna’s teenage mix of uncertainty and attitude, conveying both her lack of confidence in glow and her quietly determined spirit. Her delivery blends vulnerability with flashes of dry, slightly exasperated humour, perfectly reflecting Luna’s journey towards believing in herself.
- Robin Robin is voiced by Shash Hira (Thomas Edison’s Secret Lab, Kartoon Channel) who transforms the character into an endearingly theatrical, slightly grumpy elder of the woods. With the ‘Thespian’ air of a retired stage actor, Shash gives Robin a wonderfully expressive presence, blending dry wit with surprising tenderness.
- Bellona Butterfly is voiced in the pilot episode by Naomi McDonald, who brings warmth, authority and genuine emotional depth to the role. Naomi's performance establishes the tone and presence of Bellona beautifully as the series moves towards full production.
- Each additional episode will also feature celebrity guest voices , adding both variety and marketing reach. These guest stars, ideally with their own digital followings, will be rotated regularly, providing repeatable PR moments and allowing the team to test which voices resonate most through YouTube analytics. High-performing guest characters and their corresponding talent can be reintroduced in future episodes, deepening audience connection and extending the IP’s influencer network organically.
