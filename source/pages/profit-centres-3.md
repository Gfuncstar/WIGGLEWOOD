## Body copy
- Ownership
- The Wiggle Wood Company Limited (TWWC), wholly owned by Aimée Anderson, holds 100% of the intellectual property rights to Wiggle Wood. There are no co-owners, no third-party splits and no rights encumbrances on any element of the brand. Every contributor working on the IP, from writers and composers to designers and animators, operates under formal agreements assigning rights to the company, creating a clean, fully-documented chain of title. Wiggle Wood has been built from the outset as a single-owner asset, structured to maximise long-term IP value and protect investor returns across every monetisation channel.
- Trademark Strategy
- Wiggle Wood's trademark position is already established in its home market. The brand was registered as a UK trademark in October 2023, filed deliberately across approximately 500 categories of goods and services. This breadth was a considered strategic choice: it protects the full range of commercial activity the brand may extend into over time, from broadcast and digital content through publishing, music, toys, apparel, food and beverage, and live experiences. It gives Wiggle Wood the freedom to grow into any commercially relevant category without having to play catch-up on protection, and the registration carries the brand through to October 2028 before active use must be demonstrated at renewal.
- A phased international filing programme is now in place to extend that protection into the brand's priority commercial territories. The first phase comprises the European Union, with the anchor application planned for September 2026 to trigger a six-month priority window that preserves the same filing date across subsequent jurisdictions. Filings will then extend into the United States, Mexico, Brazil, Canada and Australia, with India and other markets to follow in line with commercial expansion. International registrations are timed to mature around late 2028 to early 2029, deliberately sequenced to be in place ahead of the merchandise launch.
- Ahead of the October 2028 UK renewal point, a category review will identify which classes are commercially active and should be retained, ensuring the renewed UK position reflects the brand's actual and intended market presence. A dedicated budget supports both the international filing programme and the active management of the IP, including monitoring for unauthorised use in priority territories and proportionate enforcement where required. The strategy is led by specialist IP counsel at Clintons.
- Copyright and Character Rights
- All characters, designs, scripts and story premises are owned outright by TWWC, with no creative collaborator retaining residual rights. The full character roster, including Cyd, Chadwick, Cosmo, Bellona Butterfly and supporting characters such as Luna Glow-worm, is wholly owned by the company and protected by the trademark and copyright strategies set out on this page.
- Music Rights
- Music is a core pillar of the Wiggle Wood IP, and TWWC owns it outright. The company holds both the publishing rights and the master rights to every song in the series. This is unusual in children's content, where music is typically co-owned with publishers or co-writers, and it represents significant long-term value as the music catalogue grows alongside the series and licensing opportunities expand into streaming, sync, live performance and consumer products.
- Compliance and Safeguarding
- Wiggle Wood is built to the highest standards of child safety and regulatory compliance. The core production team is drawn from CBeebies, widely regarded as the gold standard for safeguarding in UK children's content. The company complies with COPPA, the ICO Age Appropriate Design Code, GDPR-K, and YouTube's Made for Kids framework, with safeguarding considerations embedded in every stage of production rather than added as an afterthought.
- Two named consultants reinforce this position. Dr Jacqueline Harding, child development and education consultant, leads developmental advisory and audience insight, conducting focus groups with preschool children and reviewing scripts through the lens of the Early Years Foundation Stage. Ally Castle MBE, creative strategist and disability consultant, advises on inclusivity and representation, with particular focus on the portrayal of Chadwick, who uses an acorn wheel as a mobility aid, ensuring he is integrated naturally and respectfully within the narrative.
- Counsel
- Wiggle Wood's IP and legal strategy is led by Clintons, an award-winning entertainment law firm with more than 60 years of specialist experience in audio-visual rights, financing and licensing. Luke Gornell, Principal and head of the IP division at Clintons, is personally and substantively involved in Wiggle Wood, leading his team on day-to-day work and providing senior oversight across the trademark strategy, contributor agreements and the wider IP position.
