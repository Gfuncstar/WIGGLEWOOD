## Body copy
- Wiggle Wood is designed as a global children's entertainment brand, not simply an animated series.
- The model is straightforward. We produce premium animated content and distribute it free on YouTube, the world's largest video platform. This builds a large, loyal, and emotionally engaged global audience. That audience drives revenue across every dimension of the brand.
- The downstream commercial opportunity in children's IP is well established. Brands such as Peppa Pig (acquired for $4bn in 2019, generating over $1bn in annual merchandise revenue) and PAW Patrol (over $14bn in cumulative global retail sales since launch) demonstrate how characters that families fall in love with generate substantial enterprise value. In both cases, the content built the audience. The audience built the brand. The brand built the business.
- Wiggle Wood is being built with the same commercial architecture, but with a structural advantage those brands did not have. Rather than relying on broadcasters to reach families, Wiggle Wood goes directly to YouTube, reaching a global audience immediately, without paywalls, territorial restrictions, or broadcast schedules. YouTube's algorithm actively promotes engaging content to matched audiences, creating a discovery engine that compounds over time.
- YouTube-first does not mean YouTube-only. As Wiggle Wood's audience grows, the performance data it generates becomes a powerful negotiating tool with broadcasters and streaming platforms globally. This is the reverse window model: rather than chasing a broadcast deal from a standing start, Wiggle Wood arrives at those conversations with a proven audience, real viewing figures, and demonstrated demand. CoComelon, Ms Rachel, and Super Simple Songs all secured broadcast and streaming deals while remaining freely available on YouTube. Wiggle Wood's full broadcast and streaming strategy is set out in the Sales and Distribution section.
- As the audience grows, Wiggle Wood monetises across six core streams:
- Consumer products and merchandise
- Music streaming and publishing
- Content licensing to broadcast and streaming platforms
- Brand partnerships and retail collaborations
- Gaming and digital experiences
- Educational products and partnerships
- Each of these is covered in detail in the Profit Centres section. Together they form a diversified commercial platform in which audience scale translates directly into enterprise value, and in which Wiggle Wood retains full ownership of the IP that drives all of it.
