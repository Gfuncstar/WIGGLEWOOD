## Body copy
- Adam leads the creative development and execution of the Wiggle Wood series, shaping the storytelling voice, emotional tone, and narrative structure that define the world of Wiggle Wood. Working closely with the writers' room, directors, composers, and animation team, he oversees character development, story arcs, humour, pacing, and musical integration to ensure a distinctive, cohesive viewing experience that resonates with young audiences and supports repeat engagement. As series creative lead, Adam safeguards the authenticity and consistency of the Wiggle Wood universe on screen, and will work in close collaboration with the Chief Content Officer, once appointed, to ensure the storytelling DNA of the series can expand coherently across future formats, extensions, and platforms.
- A screenwriter and creative producer with 20 years' experience in children's and family entertainment, Adam started his career as a Henson-trained puppeteer before moving into scripting and animation production, where he says the job is just as rewarding but easier on the knees. He spent 13 years at the BBC and has more recently led creative teams at Moonbug, known for its digital-first acquisitions strategy. He has led teams across live action, animation, and video games, creating characters and stories that reach children across platforms.
- Credits include: Oddbods, Go Jetters, JoJo and Gran Gran, As You Like It at Shakespeare's Globe, Blue Peter, and Legends of MeoShín'Ké, alongside numerous animation development projects for BBC Studios Kids and Family.
- Marisa leads production on the Wiggle Wood series, overseeing schedules, budgets, and day-to-day delivery to ensure the series is produced to the highest standard, on time and on budget. She manages the interface between the creative and production teams, ensuring that the ambition of the series is matched by rigorous production planning and financial discipline. Her role is central to the smooth running of each production phase, from development through to final delivery.
- Starting her TV career in the early 2000s, Marisa has worked across a wide range of factual and non-scripted programming, from observational documentaries and access-based productions to historical drama documentaries and arts programming. Through her work with Nest Productions, she has provided Head of Production support to a wide range of companies, assisting with production management, troubleshooting live productions, and securing commissions and funding for new programming. She brings particular expertise in budgeting and financing, with a track record of finding innovative approaches to production funding.
- Selected credits include: Love in the Flesh (BBC3), Ashley Banjo: Britain in Black and White (ITV1), Amy Winehouse and Me (MTV), Inside the Vatican (BBC1/PBS/Netflix), Treasures from the Wreck of the Unbelievable (Netflix), Diana: Our Mother (ITV/HBO/RTL), Putin: The New Tsar (BBC2/Arte), The Force (Channel 4), and Our Queen/Our Queen at 90 (ITV/WW/Smithsonian).
- Hannah is the steady hand at the centre of Wiggle Wood's day to day production. As Senior Production Manager, she is the person making sure every episode lands on time, on budget and to the quality the series deserves, holding the threads of schedule, spend and team coordination so the creative work can flourish.
- A hands on Senior Production Manager with twelve years in television, Hannah heads up Nest Productions' Manchester operations, and brings experience across an unusually broad range of programming. Her work spans multi-episodic children's series in live action, animation and puppetry, as well as live theatre captures and EPKs, observational documentaries, commercials and scripted shorts. That breadth is part of what makes her such a valuable presence on Wiggle Wood, bringing the rigour of long-form children's production together with the agility she has built across genres.
- Selected credits include Sky Kids' My Friend Misty, Milkshake's The World According to Grandpa, CBeebies' Dodge's Pup School and BBC's Thames View, alongside Matthew Bourne's The Red Shoes and Swan Lake, Rock of Ages, Sister Act and The Rocky Horror Show, and observational documentaries including BBC's The Mormons Are Coming and Catching the Catfish Crime Gang.
- Originally from Crewe and now settled in Manchester for the past fifteen years, Hannah brings a rare combination of warmth and rigour to her work. She is the kind of production manager who anticipates problems before they arise, protects the creative team's focus, and keeps everyone aligned around what matters most: making something children all over the world will love.
- Mark serves as Wiggle Wood's Sustainability Advisor, overseeing the company's environmental and ethical commitments across production and operations.
- Mark Downes is one of the UK's most respected figures in sustainable media production. Chief Storyteller at Green Eyed Monster Films, he creates high-impact, low-carbon content across themes including environmental science, regenerative agriculture, ESG, biodiversity, circular economies, and climate. He is Head of Content for the Earth Prize, a member of the Expert Council for Open ESG, and former Head of Sustainability at Ecoflix, the global not-for-profit streaming platform dedicated to nature documentaries. As a lead tutor at Adgreen for two years, he explained climate science and carbon reduction to screen industry professionals, inspiring many to become agents of change within their own organisations.
- His academic credentials include a CISL qualification from Cambridge University's Business Leadership course on Sustainability, postgraduate study in the UN Sustainable Development Goals for Business at Erasmus University in Rotterdam, and Associate Membership of IEMA. He is trained by Albert/BAFTA in carbon literacy and sustainable production and is a contributor to the Purposeful Disruptors network.
- A career highlight was producing a trilogy of films with Sir David Attenborough for Generation Ocean, educating children worldwide about plastic pollution and the health of our oceans. His client work spans Visa, Nike, Beats by Dre, HSBC, the Co-op, Plastic Oceans, and Too Good to Go.
- For Wiggle Wood, Mark leads the series' sustainability commitment, ensuring every episode and series is produced with a carbon report in accordance with Albert, BAFTA's framework.
