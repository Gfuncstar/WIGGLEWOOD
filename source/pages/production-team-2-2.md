- h4: International Dubbing Director - Ray Gillon

## Body copy
- When a film moves seamlessly from one language to another, retaining not just the words but the warmth, the humour, and the emotional truth of the original, that is the work of Ray Gillon.
- Ray is one of the most experienced international dubbing directors in the animation industry, and one of the most exceptionally qualified linguists working in film. He speaks more than sixteen languages colloquially, not just fluently, but with genuine command of regional dialect, local idiom, and cultural nuance. He understands the difference between Canadian French and French French, between Colombian Spanish and Castilian, between the way a phrase lands in one territory and the very different way it lands in another. This depth of linguistic understanding is rare in any field. In the world of international dubbing, it is extraordinary.
- Over a career spanning nearly 40 years at Warner Bros, he oversaw foreign-language versions of major studio releases across up to 37 languages, working at the intersection of that linguistic precision, cultural intuition, and audio engineering. His credits span some of the most beloved properties in global entertainment, including Harry Potter, Jurassic Park, Gravity, and Dune: Part Two. Through his work at Warner Bros, Ray also directed the international dubbing of the Studio Ghibli catalogue, the celebrated Japanese animation studio behind Spirited Away and My Neighbour Totoro.
- His approach to international versioning goes far beyond translation. For large-scale animation productions, he led simultaneous voice casting across dozens of territories, building what he describes as an orchestra of voices, where every character sounds right within the social, cultural, and emotional world of a specific audience. Every choice, from casting to performance direction to phrasing, is made in service of preserving the original's intent.
- Ray has received international recognition for his work, including a Spanish Academy Award, a Turkish Golden Orange, and honours from the Polish film industry. He has worked with Dolby Laboratories and served on panels including AES Recording Competitions. He is also a thoughtful voice on the future of the industry, including the promise and the significant ethical complexity of AI-assisted dubbing.
- For Wiggle Wood, Ray will serve as International Dubbing Director, overseeing every element of the dubbing process across priority territories. The first international version will be Latin American Spanish, with further territories prioritised based on audience data from the YouTube launch. Where the English-language series demonstrates strong traction in a given territory, Wiggle Wood will work with local broadcasters to commission localised versions for those markets. Ray will lead that localisation process in each territory, overseeing script adaptation, voice casting, and dubbing direction to ensure every version carries the same warmth, emotional clarity, and musical spirit as the English original. Drawing on his experience of the contractual complexities that arise in major international animation, he will also advise on versioning-related provisions to ensure nothing is missed as the brand scales globally.
