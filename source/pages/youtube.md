## Body copy
- YouTube has opened its monetisation programme to all channels from the point of launch. The previous thresholds requiring 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours before a channel could apply to the YouTube Partner Programme (YPP) have been removed. Channels can now declare participation in the YPP and begin generating advertising revenue from their very first published video.
- For Wiggle Wood, this is a meaningful commercial advantage. Advertising income begins from day one of launch, rather than after hitting subscriber milestones that could take months to reach. Combined with Lasso Group's established relationship with YouTube and their experience managing multiple successful children's channels, Wiggle Wood is positioned to start generating platform revenue immediately and scale that income alongside growing viewership from the outset.
- YouTube operates as an advertising-funded platform. Content creators publish videos that attract audiences, and advertisers pay YouTube to reach those viewers. As a result, YouTube’s commercial success entirely depends on having and supporting as many popular channels as possible. YouTube is therefore designed to actively promote content that keeps audiences watching. Its discovery system, commonly referred to as the algorithm, analyses viewer behaviour and automatically recommends videos to people who are likely to enjoy them.
- When a channel publishes high-quality content consistently, YouTube progressively introduces that content to larger and larger audiences with similar viewing habits. For this reason, Wiggle Wood will release one episode per week across 52 weeks rather than publishing the entire series at once. This consistent release schedule works with the algorithm and allows YouTube to continually test, learn and expand the audience organically over time.
- Therefore, Wiggle Wood’s own marketing efforts will focus on directing viewers from digital platforms outside of YouTube, such as social media and digital media, to the Wiggle Wood channel, signalling strong engagement to YouTube and further accelerating organic discovery from YouTube’s algorithm.
- It is worth emphasising that consistency of release is critical: once the weekly publishing cycle begins, it must not be interrupted. Once content starts to be posted at an episode a week, we cannot stop or we kill the algorithm and weaken engagement.
- Wiggle Wood will adopt a disciplined weekly release strategy , publishing one new episode each week from launch. Consistency is critical to building momentum and longevity on YouTube. The platform’s discovery system favours channels that publish regularly and reliably, allowing it to learn which audiences respond most strongly and progressively recommend the content to more viewers over time. Interruptions to this pattern can disrupt audience growth and reduce the effectiveness of the discovery system for future episodes.
- To ensure and protect this consistency, Wiggle Wood production will begin one year ahead of the public launch , creating a full year of completed episodes before the first release. This production runway ensures that once the channel begins publishing, it can continue releasing new episodes every week without interruption. It also provides operational resilience, allowing the production team to absorb any unexpected delays while maintaining the release schedule. The objective is simple but strategically important: once Wiggle Wood begins its weekly publishing cycle, it continues without pause, allowing the channel to build sustained audience growth and long-term momentum on the platform for decades to come.
- Wiggle Wood has been purpose-built to thrive on YouTube, aligning fully with the platform’s Made for Kids environment to support strong visibility, engagement, and long-term discoverability.
- Alongside full-length 10-minute animated episodes, the channel will also publish a consistent pipeline of short-form clips, and ‘collections’ of content such as musical videos, character moments, and repurposed story segments designed to maximise discoverability and encourage repeat viewing. This mixed content strategy reflects how preschool audiences consume YouTube, where short-form content often drives discovery while longer episodes build deeper engagement and watch time.
- Episode themes will align with the most common YouTube search interests among preschool audiences, combining trending keywords with Wiggle Wood’s rich library of nature facts, emotional learning moments, and character-driven storytelling. This approach ensures that content remains both meaningful and discoverable, balancing strong SEO performance with the emotional resonance that drives repeat viewing from young audiences.
- Over time, this growing catalogue of evergreen content creates a durable digital library that continues generating views, engagement, and audience loyalty long after initial release.
- The Wiggle Wood YouTube channel will serve as the global digital home for the brand, providing a safe and trusted environment where children and families can enjoy free animated content, music, and stories while building a lasting emotional connection with the characters and world.
- Channel growth will follow a structured three-year trajectory designed to build audience scale while maintaining a healthy and commercially valuable viewer base. The business plan models approximately 15 million views in Year 1, 100 million views in Year 2, and 300 million views in Year 3 , establishing Wiggle Wood as a major preschool entertainment channel within its first few years.
- The first year will focus on building channel foundations through consistent uploads, expanding the content library, and driving early audience discovery. Because preschool audiences tend to return to familiar content rather than actively explore unknown channels, early growth will be supported through targeted external audience amplification rather than relying solely on YouTube’s algorithm.
- While YouTube will generate advertising income, its primary strategic role is to act as a powerful global discovery engine for the wider Wiggle Wood brand, driving audience engagement that supports merchandise, publishing, music, licensing, and future franchise expansion.
- By early 2025, YouTube had become the largest broadcaster on the planet, surpassing every individual media company, including Netflix, in US TV screen share. In April 2025, YouTube captured 12.4% of all US television viewing. Globally, audiences now watch over one billion hours of YouTube on television screens every single day, and YouTube TV has 9.4 million paying subscribers.
- This growth is no longer driven by mobile clips. YouTube is rapidly transforming into a full-scale streaming service for the living room, introducing a Shows interface that mirrors Netflix and Disney+ in presentation and functionality. Channels are being reimagined as polished streaming hubs with season browsing, cinematic artwork, and scheduled episode shelves.
- Wiggle Wood is designed for exactly this environment. Its 10-minute episodic format, original music, and consistent weekly release structure are already built to the standard of premium streaming content. Launching into the Shows format gives the series cinematic visibility on smart TVs, encourages the kind of repeat family viewing that builds long-term loyalty, and positions the channel for broadcast-style advertising revenue and brand partnerships at OTT pricing levels.
- Wiggle Wood enters this living-room era at the moment YouTube is capturing record market share and advertiser attention. That timing is a deliberate strategic advantage.
