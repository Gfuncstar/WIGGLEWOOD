## Body copy
- Wiggle Wood will launch using a digital-first global distribution strategy, releasing each season as 52 weekly episodes on YouTube, supported by short-form content designed to drive discovery, engagement, and audience growth.
- YouTube is now the largest broadcaster in the world and the most significant global platform for children's content, offering immediate international reach and powerful algorithm-driven discovery. The platform's dominance in the children's market has been widely acknowledged across the industry. In 2025, Sky publicly announced that it would no longer commission original children's programming, citing the shift of young audiences to YouTube.
- Launching on YouTube allows Wiggle Wood to reach families globally from day one, while building a direct audience relationship without traditional distribution barriers. Performance will be measured primarily through viewership rather than subscriber numbers, reflecting best practice in digital media.
- The series' digital distribution and channel strategy will be managed by Lasso Group, one of the most experienced YouTube growth and distribution teams working in children's content globally.
- Wiggle Wood's international distribution strategy has been developed by Distribution and Sales Consultant Caroline Stern, drawing on her extensive experience across international film and television sales. It is designed to maximise commercial return, manage risk, and retain strategic control at every stage of growth.
- Rather than releasing without restriction in pursuit of the highest aggregate view count, Wiggle Wood will follow a deliberate, phased rollout. This ensures marketing resources are concentrated where they are most effective, that performance data can be collected and acted upon, and that the brand is established with real commercial strength before entering new markets.
- Phase 1: Year One - English and Spanish
- The initial release will be in English and Spanish only. The Spanish version will be a Latin American Spanish dub, reflecting the most widely understood and culturally neutral Spanish dialect across all Spanish-speaking territories globally. These two languages represent the largest accessible preschool audiences globally, enabling focused marketing investment with maximum return. Spain will be revisited as a dedicated market in a later phase, with a Castilian Spanish dub developed once the Latin American Spanish version has established performance data.
- Neither the English nor Spanish release will be geoblocked, meaning the content remains accessible worldwide. Viewership from non-target territories will provide useful organic data, while marketing spend is concentrated where it will build the strongest early audience base. Phase 1 is primarily about building the channel, collecting performance data, and establishing the brand with confidence before expanding.
- International Dubbing and Localisation
- Producing a dubbed version requires more than translation. For a series built on emotional resonance, every dubbed version must carry the same warmth, cultural meaning, and character intent as the English original.
- Why Latin American Spanish
- Spanish is the second most widely spoken language in the world, with over 500 million native speakers across more than 20 countries. But Spanish is not a single language. There are significant dialectal differences across territories, and Castilian Spanish, spoken in Spain, can carry associations that create distance for Latin American audiences. Choosing the right Spanish for a first international dub is a genuine strategic decision.
- Latin American Spanish, specifically the Colombian and Venezuelan variants, is considered the most neutral and widely understood across all Spanish-speaking territories globally, including Spain. A single Latin American Spanish dub therefore gives Wiggle Wood the broadest possible reach across the full Spanish-speaking world from a single version.
- Data-Driven Territory Prioritisation
- Beyond Spanish, language and territory decisions for subsequent dubbing phases will be driven by audience data from the YouTube launch. Where the English-language series demonstrates strong organic viewership in a territory, Wiggle Wood will commission a localised version for that market and open broadcaster conversations. Dubbing investment follows demonstrated demand rather than prediction.
- International Dubbing Director: Ray Gillon
- International dubbing quality will be overseen by Ray Gillon, one of the most experienced international dubbing directors in the animation industry. With nearly 40 years at Warner Bros overseeing foreign-language versions of major properties including Harry Potter and Studio Ghibli across up to 37 languages, Ray (who speaks 16 languages colloquially) brings exceptional expertise to this role. He will oversee script adaptation, voice casting, and dubbing direction across all territories, ensuring every version of Wiggle Wood reaches children with the same emotional clarity and musical spirit as the English original.
- Phase 2: Year Two - European Expansion and Broadcast Conversations
- Phase 2 builds on the audience and data accumulated in Year 1, expanding into key European territories including Germany, France, the Netherlands, Italy, the Nordics, Poland and beyond. British preschool shows export particularly well across Europe, and these premium markets are prioritised for their revenue potential.
- During Phase 2, Wiggle Wood will also begin conversations with international broadcasters and streaming platforms. Potential partners include multi-territory platforms such as Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, Disney+, and Paramount+, alongside leading regional broadcasters including TF1 and Canal+ (France), ARD's KiKa and ZDF Junior (Germany), Rai YoYo (Italy), and SVT Barn (Sweden).
- China will also enter strategic focus during Phase 2. YouTube does not operate in China, making it one of the world's largest children's markets that requires a dedicated licensing strategy. Initial approaches will focus on securing a competitive exclusive deal with one of the major Chinese platforms, including iQIYI, Youku, and Tudou. The value of any deal will be informed by performance data from previously released territories. Peppa Pig's experience in China is instructive: released there in 2015, it accumulated more than 5.4 billion views within nine months across these platforms and remains a top-performing international property there today.
- Phase 3: Year Three and Beyond - Global Exploitation
- Phase 3 focuses on full global exploitation of the IP, managing and reviewing existing licences while expanding into secondary markets across Eastern Europe, the Middle East, and Asia. In these territories, Licensing and Merchandising value significantly outweighs broadcast licence fees, and distribution decisions will be made in close coordination with the L&M team to maximise total commercial return. Where appropriate, local partners may be appointed to manage rights, promotion, and merchandising coherently within specific regions.
- Conventional wisdom holds that making content available for free online eliminates the possibility of selling it to subscription platforms. For Wiggle Wood, the opposite may prove true.
- There are established precedents in the preschool space of YouTube-first content achieving such significant audience scale that streaming platforms have actively sought to licence it, even while it remains freely available. CoComelon, Super Simple Songs, and Ms Rachel have all secured Netflix deals despite remaining on YouTube. Moonbug has successfully placed CoComelon, Blippi, and Little Baby Bum on Deutsche Telekom's MagentaTV while retaining free access on YouTube.
- This reverse window dynamic is unique to the preschool category, where platform familiarity and parent trust drive demand rather than exclusivity. Strong performance data from Wiggle Wood's YouTube channel will position the brand to pursue these opportunities from a position of demonstrated audience strength rather than speculation.
- While YouTube forms the central distribution hub, Wiggle Wood will expand onto additional digital platforms once the first 10 to 12 episodes have been released and initial channel performance established.
- The focus will be on major platforms including Prime Video (non-exclusive), Tubi, and Roku, alongside leading dedicated children's OTT channels. Kidoodle operates a dedicated preschool swim lane featuring properties including Numberblocks, Thomas and Friends, and Colour Crew. HappyKids provides safe, ad-supported streaming across web, app, Roku, Amazon Fire TV, Apple TV, and smart TVs.
- The YouTube channel remains the definitive home of Wiggle Wood, housing all episodes, compilations, and additional content. Distribution across other platforms will be managed to drive brand recognition and discovery without diverting audiences away from the primary channel.
- Airlines
- All commercial airlines require children's programming and typically prioritise content that younger audiences already recognise and enjoy. Initial approaches will be made to airline buyers in Phase 1 release territories, specifically the UK, Ireland, United States, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, Spain, and Latin American territories.
- Airline buyers are not purely driven by viewing metrics; they seek a varied offering that appeals to parents as well as children. Approaches will begin as soon as favourable YouTube viewing figures or market awareness through press and publicity can be demonstrated. Airline licences are typically structured on six to twelve-month terms, with the objective of securing ongoing rolling deals across multiple seasons.
- Educational Sales
- As preschools and schools increasingly licence programming for classroom use, Wiggle Wood will pursue educational distribution in each territory as part of the wider rollout.
- The current leader in providing content to educational institutions is Kanopy, a streaming platform operating across all English-speaking and Spanish-speaking territories and expanding globally. Through its Kanopy Kids service, it offers strong visibility for a range of preschool programming. Deals are typically non-exclusive and structured on a revenue-share basis. As Wiggle Wood rolls out internationally, school licensing opportunities will be pursued in parallel, supported by teacher resource packs and classroom materials developed alongside selected episodes.
- Music is a core commercial asset within the Wiggle Wood IP, and its distribution extends well beyond the animated episodes themselves.
- With ten original songs per season building into a growing catalogue over time, Wiggle Wood music will be made available across streaming platforms including Spotify Kids, Apple Music, and Amazon Music, as well as smart speaker ecosystems where young children increasingly control their own listening. The potential to adapt original songs into alternative formats, such as instrumental lullabies and sleep versions, creates additional discoverability and monetisation opportunities across these platforms.
- Wiggle Wood is also exploring hardware partnerships with Tonies and Yoto, two of the leading screen-free interactive audio platforms for young children, representing a further opportunity to bring the brand into family homes in a trusted, parent-endorsed format.
- As a natural companion to the series, Wiggle Wood will develop a dedicated sleep and mindfulness podcast designed for preschool audiences and their families. Wiggle Wood Winddown Time will feature short meditations for daily resets, gentle observation activities for quiet moments, and longer guided meditations and stories for bedtime.
