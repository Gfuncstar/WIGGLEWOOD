The Animation Series

Synopsis

Three young caterpillars, one wise butterfly, and a woodland full of friends to help and feelings to wiggle through.

The Story

Our story follows three young caterpillars, affectionately known as the Wigglers: Cyd and Cosmo, two bold and curious girls, and Chadwick, their high-spirited best friend.

Together they live at Caterpillar Cubby, a branch on The Great Oak in the heart of Wiggle Wood, where they go on hilarious adventures, meet other animals in need of help, and discover nature’s best kept secrets.

In each episode, they meet a new friend and uncover some of life’s great lessons, overcoming challenges and realising that individuality, diversity and failure should be celebrated, not feared.

Guided by Bellona Butterfly, the Wigglers come to understand that it is their differences and idiosyncrasies that make them successful. Wise Bellona reminds them that these lessons are crucial on their journey to becoming butterflies, when they will need great strength to fly. On that important journey, above all else, kindness and love must be their north star.

The Wiggle Wood Format

Every 10-minute episode of Wiggle Wood follows the same carefully considered structure, a repeatable emotional journey designed to resonate with preschool children and build genuine developmental value through storytelling, music, and reflection.

1
The Emotional Trigger

Each episode begins with one or more of the Wigglers experiencing a moment of emotional difficulty: worry, frustration, anxiety, anger, or uncertainty. These are feelings every young child recognises. The episode begins where the child already is.

2
Wiggle It Out

Rather than moving straight into action, the Wigglers pause and wiggle it out. Wiggle It Out is a recurring musical centrepiece of every episode: an uplifting, transitional music video moment in which the characters sing, move, and release their feelings. It is both emotionally purposeful and enormously watchable, a moment that children will return to, sing along with, and carry with them. As the song ends, the Wigglers find themselves somewhere new in the world of Wiggle Wood, ready for what comes next.

3
The Guest Character

In this new location, the Wigglers encounter a woodland resident or passing animal facing a problem that mirrors their own. By listening to that character, offering comfort, and trying to help, the Wigglers begin to see their own situation differently. The shift in perspective is not taught. It is discovered. This is the emotional core of the format: children learn through empathy, not instruction. Success is not always guaranteed, but teamwork, empathy, and listening lead the Wigglers closer to what really matters.

4
The Return to Caterpillar Cubby

The Wigglers return home to Caterpillar Cubby, their treehouse in the heart of Wiggle Wood. With Bellona, their warm and wise caregiver who is present throughout every episode, they reflect on what they have experienced and put their new understanding into words. The episode closes with a reflective song that reinforces the emotional arc of the story, often led by Bellona, whose musical presence creates consistency across the series and supports future music video, playlist, and licensing opportunities. The emotional lesson is gently consolidated, never prescribed.

5
The Mindfulness Moment

Every episode ends with a short mindfulness segment during the credits, offering simple, age-appropriate meditations focused on self-worth, calm, and gratitude. These gentle moments are designed to support positive mental health in young viewers and to ease the transition between episodes or toward bedtime, leaving children settled, reassured, and ready for what comes next.

This structure was not arrived at by chance. It was developed with direct input from Dr. Jacqueline Harding, one of the UK’s foremost authorities in early childhood development and brain science. Dr. Harding has specifically highlighted the importance of the transitional moment at the heart of every episode. For young children, big feelings such as worry, frustration, and overwhelm can feel impossible to move through. The Wiggle Wood format is built around exactly that movement: from being consumed by a feeling, through a moment of transition, towards a shift in perception. Children are not simply shown how to feel better. They are guided through the process of getting there, which is precisely what early years developmental science tells us they need. The format is also built to scale. Every episode follows the same architecture, which means the creative process is consistent, the production pipeline is efficient, and the audience always knows what to expect and what to look forward to.

Episode Stories

A glimpse into the storytelling

The following episodes offer a glimpse into the storytelling world of Wiggle Wood. Each story is built around a simple emotional truth, brought to life through character, humour, and the natural world. Twenty episodes have been developed at springboard stage, with the full document available to download in the Documents section. The five stories below represent the breadth and emotional range of the series, with the remaining 52 episodes per season developed as we move into pre-production.

Pilot Episode

Show Your Glow

Cyd is determined to make the perfect gift for Bellona’s Butterversary, but when the Wigglers meet shy Luna Glow-worm, who refuses to shine because she believes her light is not bright enough, Cyd begins to see perfection differently. Through friendship and song, the Wigglers discover that beauty is not about being flawless, but about being proud of who you are and letting your own unique light shine. Together, they learn that your best is beautiful.

Episode 2

Don’t Yuck my Dung

Bruno the dung beetle needs help rolling his dung ball, but Cosmo is not exactly thrilled about touching poo. Bruno sets about teaching the Wigglers about the wonders of dung, and that just because you do not like something, it does not mean everyone feels the same way. And that is perfectly fine.

Episode 3

Hog Bugs

A gang of greedy, gregarious locusts are passing through Wiggle Wood, intent on eating everything in sight. With a little help from Bellona, the Wigglers show the bothersome bugs that there is more joy in sharing than in gobbling everything up.

Episode 4

Noisy Neighbours

Cosmo is busy building a new swing for the Wigglers, but she cannot concentrate with Chadwick and Cyd holding an impromptu dance party nearby. She heads off to wiggle out her frustration and bumps into Robin Robin, who is at his wit’s end. His neighbour, Oliver Owl, loves to sing through the night, and nocturnal Robin simply cannot sleep. Practical Cosmo tries everything, from earplugs to soundproofing Robin’s tree hollow, but nothing works. The answer turns out to be far simpler: Robin plucks up the courage to talk to Oliver, who is immediately understanding and happy to keep it down. Cosmo realises she could do the same with Chadwick and Cyd. She does, they oblige, and since Robin is now wide awake, why not have a dance party together before Cosmo gets back to her swing?

Episode 5

Strong As Silk!

Gung-ho Chadwick has got himself into quite a pickle, and Cyd and Cosmo are going to need a rope to get him out. Sansi Silkworm offers up her thread, but Chadwick cannot believe something so delicate could possibly hold. He is about to learn that looks can be deceiving, and that things — and people — are often far stronger than they appear.

A wholesome, music-led animated series promoting kindness, diversity and inclusion. A brand built to last.
