Business Model

How Wiggle Wood Generates Revenue

Wiggle Wood is designed as a global children’s entertainment brand, not simply an animated series. The model is straightforward. We produce premium animated content and distribute it free on YouTube, the world’s largest video platform. This builds a large, loyal, and emotionally engaged global audience. That audience drives revenue across every dimension of the brand.

The downstream commercial opportunity in children’s IP is well established. Brands such as Peppa Pig (brand value approximately $1.7bn USD) and PAW Patrol (brand value over $15bn USD) demonstrate how characters that families fall in love with generate substantial enterprise value. In both cases, the content built the audience. The audience built the brand. The brand built the business.

Wiggle Wood is being built with the same commercial architecture, but with a structural advantage those brands did not have. Rather than relying on broadcasters to reach families, Wiggle Wood goes directly to YouTube, reaching a global audience immediately, without paywalls, territorial restrictions, or broadcast schedules. YouTube’s algorithm actively promotes engaging content to matched audiences, creating a discovery engine that compounds over time.

YouTube-first does not mean YouTube-only. As Wiggle Wood’s audience grows, the performance data it generates becomes a powerful negotiating tool with broadcasters and streaming platforms globally. This is the reverse window model: rather than chasing a broadcast deal from a standing start, Wiggle Wood arrives at those conversations with a proven audience, real viewing figures, and demonstrated demand. CoComelon, Ms Rachel, and Super Simple Songs all secured broadcast and streaming deals while remaining freely available on YouTube.

Industry Trends & Opportunities

The children’s content landscape is undergoing a profound shift. YouTube has won the children’s audience, and the industry has accepted it. Not as a passing disruption, but as a permanent structural shift. Every major broadcaster is now responding to a world in which young children watch YouTube first, and television is no longer their default.

In a move that has sent shockwaves through the UK’s creative industry, in July 2025 Sky announced it will no longer commission original programming for children, opting instead to licence cheaper, pre-made series. With Channel 5’s Milkshake operating on limited budgets and the BBC now standing as the only major commissioner of UK children’s content, the market for original, values-led programming is rapidly narrowing.

At the same time, demand from families has never been higher. Over 4 million children in the UK are living in poverty, and for many households, YouTube is the most accessible platform for children’s entertainment. But while the content is free and abundant, much of that content is unregulated, addictive, and lacking the care, creativity and emotional nourishment that young minds deserve.

This creates a rare window of opportunity for independent creators like Wiggle Wood. Historically, a new global preschool IP tends to emerge every four to six years. Major breakout brands include Peppa Pig (2004), PAW Patrol (2013), CoComelon (2015), and Bluey (2019), each achieving significant global scale through distinctive storytelling and commercial strategies. Industry experts widely recognise that the market is now due the next major original preschool brand, particularly as reliance on legacy franchises continues to increase. Wiggle Wood is designed specifically for this window of opportunity.

Profit Centres

Wiggle Wood is designed from the outset as a multi-revenue global children’s brand. While YouTube enables the series to reach large international audiences quickly, the majority of long-term enterprise value is created through the commercial ecosystem that grows around the characters and stories. As the audience expands, Wiggle Wood monetises its global reach through a range of scalable profit centres including consumer products and merchandise, music and soundtracks, brand partnerships and retail collaborations, gaming and interactive entertainment, digital experiences and emerging platforms, and educational products and learning partnerships. Together, these revenue streams transform audience engagement into a diversified commercial platform, allowing families to bring Wiggle Wood into their homes, schools, and everyday lives while building long-term brand value.

Merchandise & Consumer Products

Consumer products and merchandise represent the largest commercial opportunity for most successful children’s brands. For many of the world’s most successful children’s properties, the majority of brand value is generated through merchandise and licensing rather than the original television or streaming content itself. Categories include toys and plush, publishing, apparel, home and bedroom products, school and everyday products, accessories, and healthy food and snack licensing.

Music

Music is a central part of the Wiggle Wood storytelling experience and a significant long-term commercial opportunity. Children’s music performs exceptionally strongly on digital platforms. Revenue streams include streaming across Spotify, Apple Music and Amazon Music, YouTube music streams, music publishing income, sync licensing, soundtrack albums, and music-driven consumer products. Wiggle Wood’s YouTube distribution partner, The Lasso Group, played a key role in the global growth of children’s music properties such as Baby Shark on YouTube.

Gaming

Gaming represents a significant and fast-growing commercial opportunity for children’s IP, with tablet-based games offering preschool audiences an immersive, interactive way to engage with the characters and worlds they love beyond the screen. Wiggle Wood is developing a tablet-based game designed specifically for the preschool age group, in partnership with Territory Studio, one of the world’s leading specialist gaming and creative studios.

Brand Partner & Retail Collaborations

Strategic brand partnerships and retail collaborations can significantly accelerate both the visibility and commercial reach of Wiggle Wood. As Wiggle Wood’s audience grows through YouTube, the brand will pursue selected retail partnerships that combine licensing, product distribution, and joint marketing initiatives. The Wiggle Wood team already has relationships with senior marketing leaders across major retail groups, including Boots UK, part of Walgreens Boots Alliance.

Aviation & Travel Partnerships

Airlines represent a distinctive and high-value partnership opportunity for Wiggle Wood. Wiggle Wood is exploring a strategic partnership with British Airways that would operate across multiple touchpoints: in-flight entertainment, a children’s club partnership, a character lending programme for young passengers, and in-flight merchandise sales. While British Airways is the anchor partnership being explored, Wiggle Wood’s ambition extends to airline partnerships across multiple carriers.

Brand Sponsorship

Brand sponsorship represents a meaningful secondary revenue stream, sitting alongside the primary commercial ecosystem of YouTube monetisation, music, licensing, and consumer products. The anchor sponsorship opportunity is an integrated audio partnership: a single category-exclusive presenting partner across the full Wiggle Wood audio ecosystem, encompassing the Winddown Time bedtime audio series, original album and lullaby releases, and parent-facing music campaigns across platforms including Spotify Kids, Amazon Music Kids, and dedicated family audio hardware such as Yoto and Tonies.

Broadcast Licensing

Secondary licensing of Wiggle Wood content to traditional broadcasters and SVOD platforms once the YouTube audience is established. Wiggle Wood’s full broadcast and streaming strategy delivers phased international rollout, with European territories in Phase 2 and global exploitation in Phase 3.

Education

As preschools and schools increasingly licence programming for classroom use, Wiggle Wood will pursue educational distribution in each territory as part of the wider rollout, supported by teacher resource packs and classroom materials developed alongside selected episodes. Dr Jacqueline Harding has confirmed that Wiggle Wood maps directly onto the UK Early Years Foundation Stage, providing a clear and credible foundation for classroom use across the UK and in equivalent curricula internationally.

YouTube Strategy

YouTube has opened its monetisation programme to all channels from the point of launch. The previous thresholds requiring 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours before a channel could apply to the YouTube Partner Programme (YPP) have been removed. Channels can now declare participation in the YPP and begin generating advertising revenue from their very first published video.

For Wiggle Wood, this is a meaningful commercial advantage. Advertising income begins from day one of launch, rather than after hitting subscriber milestones that could take months to reach. Combined with Lasso Group’s established relationship with YouTube and their experience managing multiple successful children’s channels, Wiggle Wood is positioned to start generating platform revenue immediately and scale that income alongside growing viewership from the outset.

YouTube operates as an advertising-funded platform. Content creators publish videos that attract audiences, and advertisers pay YouTube to reach those viewers. As a result, YouTube’s commercial success entirely depends on having and supporting as many popular channels as possible. YouTube is therefore designed to actively promote content that keeps audiences watching. Its discovery system, commonly referred to as the algorithm, analyses viewer behaviour and automatically recommends videos to people who are likely to enjoy them.

When a channel publishes high-quality content consistently, YouTube progressively introduces that content to larger and larger audiences with similar viewing habits. For this reason, Wiggle Wood will release one episode per week across 52 weeks rather than publishing the entire series at once. This consistent release schedule works with the algorithm and allows YouTube to continually test, learn and expand the audience organically over time.

Wiggle Wood’s own marketing efforts will focus on directing viewers from digital platforms outside of YouTube, such as social media and digital media, to the Wiggle Wood channel, signalling strong engagement to YouTube and further accelerating organic discovery from YouTube’s algorithm. Consistency of release is critical: once the weekly publishing cycle begins, it must not be interrupted.

By early 2025, YouTube had become the largest broadcaster on the planet, surpassing every individual media company, including Netflix, in US TV screen share. In April 2025, YouTube captured 12.4% of all US television viewing. Globally, audiences now watch over one billion hours of YouTube on television screens every single day. YouTube is rapidly transforming into a full-scale streaming service for the living room, introducing a Shows interface that mirrors Netflix and Disney+ in presentation and functionality. Wiggle Wood is designed for exactly this environment.

Sales and Distribution

Wiggle Wood will launch using a digital-first global distribution strategy, releasing each season as 52 weekly episodes on YouTube, supported by short-form content designed to drive discovery, engagement, and audience growth.

YouTube is now the largest broadcaster in the world and the most significant global platform for children’s content, offering immediate international reach and powerful algorithm-driven discovery. Launching on YouTube allows Wiggle Wood to reach families globally from day one, while building a direct audience relationship without traditional distribution barriers.

Wiggle Wood’s international distribution strategy has been developed by Distribution and Sales Consultant Caroline Stern, drawing on her extensive experience across international film and television sales. Rather than releasing without restriction in pursuit of the highest aggregate view count, Wiggle Wood will follow a deliberate, phased rollout. Phase 1 covers English and Spanish. Phase 2 expands into key European territories and begins broadcaster conversations. Phase 3 focuses on full global exploitation of the IP.

Conventional wisdom holds that making content available for free online eliminates the possibility of selling it to subscription platforms. For Wiggle Wood, the opposite may prove true. CoComelon, Super Simple Songs, and Ms Rachel have all secured Netflix deals despite remaining on YouTube. This reverse window dynamic is unique to the preschool category, where platform familiarity and parent trust drive demand rather than exclusivity.

Marketing Strategy

Wiggle Wood has been designed from the outset to scale rapidly and reach a global family audience. Marketing is therefore a core strategic growth driver, supported by a dedicated budget and long-term brand-building approach to accelerate awareness, drive audience acquisition, and establish Wiggle Wood as a leading international preschool franchise quickly.

In the early stages, the marketing strategy will focus on driving external traffic (parents and caregivers) to YouTube through a combination of digital adverts, talent amplification, social media promotion, and selective collaborations with established creators and children’s brands. Key creative talent attached to Wiggle Wood brings significant existing reach across major platforms. Musical Director BRIT and Grammy-nominated Jax Jones has 2.5 million followers across YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram, while actress Corinna Brown, who voices Chadwick, brings an engaged audience of more than 500,000 followers on Instagram.

Wiggle Wood’s paid social strategy is managed by ViVV, a specialist digital marketing agency with deep experience scaling children’s and family brands across social platforms. Total three-year paid social investment is planned at between £787,500 and £1,390,500 depending on organic performance. Alongside macro talent promotion, Wiggle Wood will also activate large-scale micro-influencer engagement through Hypefy, an AI-powered creator platform with access to more than 53 million content creators worldwide.

The Financial Model

Wiggle Wood has been structured to deploy capital efficiently while building a scalable content library capable of generating long-term recurring revenue across animation, music, consumer products, publishing, and licensing. The section below outlines the funding structure, financial projections and investment mechanics. A detailed financial model is available for review in our data room.

Investment Structure

Wiggle Wood offers investors a net profit participation interest in the IP rather than equity in The Wiggle Wood Company. Investors do not hold shares in the business. Instead, they receive a proportional share of net profits generated by Wiggle Wood across all revenue streams, in perpetuity. Returns begin as the series generates income and continue for as long as the IP performs, with no fixed exit event or defined end date. In the event of a future acquisition of the Wiggle Wood IP, investors participate in the proceeds in proportion to their net profit interest. This structure aligns investor returns directly with the commercial performance of the IP — a long-term, recurring income opportunity rather than a speculative equity stake dependent on a future liquidity event.

Enterprise Investment Scheme (EIS)

Wiggle Wood has received EIS advance assurance from HMRC, making it eligible for investment under the UK Enterprise Investment Scheme. For qualifying UK taxpayers this provides meaningful tax advantages, including 30% income tax relief on the amount invested, exemption from capital gains tax on returns after a minimum three-year holding period, loss relief allowing any losses to be offset against income or capital gains, and CGT deferral relief on gains reinvested through EIS.

EIS eligibility is subject to individual circumstances and applies to qualifying UK taxpayers only. Investors based outside the UK or paying tax in other jurisdictions should seek independent advice.

Production Security & Completion Bond

Wiggle Wood’s production is backed by a completion bond provided by Paterson James, the UK’s specialist completion guarantor for animation. A completion bond guarantees to investors that the production will be filmed, completed, and delivered to a high technical standard by an agreed date. Should production exceed its budget, the bulk of any over-costs become the responsibility of the Completion Guarantor rather than the investor.

Paterson James’s involvement also brings the hands-on animation expertise of Bob Thompson, who oversees production on behalf of the underwriter throughout the process.
